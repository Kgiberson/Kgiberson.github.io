Hi, I'm Kari

I am a web developer, based in the Inner Sunset neighborhood of San Francisco. I love potato pizza (don't call it weird until you try it), spending extended amounts of time in nature, and playing with code. Coffee gets me out of bed in the morning, but I'm most energized and inspired by great ideas.

Before I began coding, I was working as a farm intern, harvesting vegetables for a CSA program in Denver, CO. I loved getting my hands dirty and experiencing first hand the life-cycle of the farm, the early morning dew on the zucchini plant leaves, the taste of a freshly picked cherry tomato, the smell of cilantro as you walked past the herb section.

Farming to coding... the logical next step, right? It's true, there are lifestyle differences. My computer doesn't make my hands smell like tomatoes and my jeans aren't consistently covered in soil, but I'd argue that farming and coding have more in common than you might think. Both rely heavily on effective problem solving, the mixture of analytical and creative thinking that I crave most in my every day.
